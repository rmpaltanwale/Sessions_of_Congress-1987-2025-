The 106th US Congress met from January 3, 1999 to January 3, 2001, in the last two years of Bill Clinton's presidency.

The appropriation of the seats to each party was based on the results of the 1998 midterms (which took place during the middle of Bill Clinton's second term).

Senate composition

The total number of seats was 100.

The Senate had an overall Republican majority. Its presiding officer was Vice President Al Gore.

There were 45 Democrats, 55 Republicans and 0 Independents.

House of Representatives composition

The total number of seats was 435.

The House had an overall Republican majority. Its presiding officer was Speaker of the House Dennis Hastert.

There were 211 Democrats, 223 Republicans and 1 Independent.
