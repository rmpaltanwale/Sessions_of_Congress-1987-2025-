The 108th US Congress met from January 3, 2003 to January 3, 2005, in the third and fourth years of George W Bush's presidency.

The appropriation of the seats to each party was based on the results of the 2002 midterms (which took place during the middle of George W Bush's first term).

Senate composition

The total number of seats was 100.

The Senate had an overall Republican majority. Its presiding officer was Vice President Dick Cheney.

There were 48 Democrats, 52 Republicans and 1 Independent.

House of Representatives composition

The total number of seats was 435.

The House had an overall Republican majority. Its presiding officer was Speaker of the House Dennis Hastert.

There were 205 Democrats, 229 Republicans and 1 Independent.
