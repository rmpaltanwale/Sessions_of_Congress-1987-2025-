The 110th US Congress met from January 3, 2007 to January 3, 2009, in the last two years of George W. Bush's presidency.

The appropriation of the seats to each party was based on the results of the 2006 midterms (which took place during the middle of George W. Bush's second term).

Senate composition

The total number of seats was 100.

The Senate had an overall Democratic majority. Its presiding officer was Vice President Dick Cheney.

There were 49 Democrats, 49 Republicans and 2 Independents. Both Independents caucused with the Democrats, bringing up the Democratic total to 51 seats thus giving them a majority in the Senate.

House of Representatives composition

The total number of seats was 435.

The House had an overall Democratic majority. Its presiding officer was Speaker of the House Nancy Pelosi.

There were 233 Democrats, 202 Republicans and 0 Independents.
