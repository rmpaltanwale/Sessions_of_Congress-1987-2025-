The 115th US Congress met from January 3, 2017 to January 3, 2019, in the first two years of Donald Trump's first presidency.

The appropriation of the seats to each party was based on the results of the 2016 Congressional elections (which took place at the same time as the election of Donald Trump as President).

Senate composition

The total number of seats was 100.

The Senate had an overall Republican majority. Its presiding officer was Vice President Mike Pence.

There were 47 Democrats, 51 Republicans and 2 Independents.

House of Representatives composition

The total number of seats was 435.

The House had an overall Republican majority. Its presiding officer was Speaker of the House Paul Ryan.

There were 194 Democrats, 241 Republicans and 0 Independents.
