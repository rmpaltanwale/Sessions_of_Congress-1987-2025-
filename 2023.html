The 118th US Congress met from January 3, 2023 to January 3, 2025, in the final two years of Joe Biden's presidency.

The appropriation of the seats to each party was based on the results of the 2022 midterms (which took place during the middle of Joe Biden's term).

Senate composition

The total number of seats was 100.

The Senate had an overall Democratic majority (The 4 Independents caucused with the 47 Democrats, bringing up the Democratic total up to 51 as opposed to the 49 Republicans.) Its presiding officer was Vice President Kamala Harris.

There were 47 Democrats, 49 Republicans and 4 Independents.

House of Representatives composition

The total number of seats was 435.

The House had an overall Republicans majority. Its presiding officer was Speaker of the House Kevin McCarthy, who served from January 2023. However, due to a motion to vacate him passing the House, he was removed from the Speakership in October 2023 and was temporarily succeeded by Patrick Henry, until the election of Mike Johnson as Speaker the very same month. Johnson went on to serve out the remainder of McCarthy's term as Speaker before being elected to a full term of his own in 2025.

There were 213 Democrats, 222 Republicans and 0 Independents.
